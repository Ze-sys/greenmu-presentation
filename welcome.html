The Problem Formulation Process (PFP) is a practical, structured approach for tackling complex engineering and innovation challenges. Instead of trying to solve a big, tangled problem all at once, PFP helps you break it down into smaller, clearly-defined issues that can be addressed more effectively.

PFP guides you to identify and organize three types of problems:

  • Contradictions: Where two requirements or effects are in conflict, and improving one makes the other worse.
  • Probing Leads: Open-ended questions or areas that need further investigation to understand the system or find new opportunities.
  • Secondary Issues: Additional problems that arise as a consequence of addressing the main issues, or that are discovered during analysis.

These are the main deliverables of the PFP. By making these issues explicit, the process sets the stage for focused, creative problem solving in later steps.
